# Trademark search baseline

Snapshots of public trademark database searches for "Wayword" and the
phonetically-similar "Wayward". Kept for our own records — useful as a
reference point if a question or dispute ever arises, and as a baseline to
re-run searches against in future.

**Strong caveat:** this is a layperson reading of public databases. It is
not legal advice. If you ever want to file or get a formal opinion, talk
to an Australian IP attorney.

---

## 2026-05-20 — Initial post-viral search

Context: Wayword went mini-viral on Tumblr 2026-05-18 → 2026-05-20 (~638
reblogs in 3 days). We've made no commercial plans, but did the searches
defensively to know whether anyone holds conflicting marks.

### Top-line

- **No live "WAYWORD" registrations** in either Australia (IP Australia)
  or the US (USPTO). The two historical US "WAYWORD" applications are
  both dead/cancelled.
- **"Wayward" is registered in many classes** (it's a common English
  word), but live registrations in the directly-relevant classes
  (9 = software, 41 = entertainment/games) are scattered compound marks
  in other niches, not bare-name conflicts.
- **No conflict that looks like a real blocker for a free word game.** A
  deep-pocketed holder could *try* to oppose on phonetic-similarity
  grounds; legal merits look weak. The risk is more "cost of defending"
  than "you'd lose."

### Phonetic-similarity check: "WAYWARD"

Live in classes 9 or 41 (the relevant ones):

  16, 35, 41 — REGISTERED. Compound mark.

Other live AU "wayward" marks all in unrelated classes (clothing,
beverages, food, transport, beauty services, vehicles).

### Assessment

1. **Exact name is clear** in the two jurisdictions that matter most for
   us (AU + US). No live "WAYWORD" mark to conflict with.
2. **"Wayward" is well-trodden but scattered.** None of the live
   registrations look like word games. The closest game analog
   (WAYWARD SOULS, US) is dead.
3. **The one nominal AU concern** — Random House Group's "WAYWARD TxF"
   in our exact classes — is a compound mark. Their enforceable scope is
   the compound, not "WAYWARD" alone. A challenge against bare "Wayword"
   in a word-game context would have weak legal merit.
4. **The most interesting nuisance signal** is that wayword.com has been
   held by an opaque registrant since 1998. Not a trademark issue, but
   worth knowing if someone ever tries to monetise the .com.

### Action items at the time of this snapshot

- **No urgent filing.** Searches are clean enough; we're not at risk
  today.
- **Do not file pre-emptively.** ~$500 AUD for IP Australia in classes 9
  + 41 is not worth it until there's a specific trigger (acquisition
  conversation, threat letter, commercial plans).
- **If circumstances change** — file in Australia first, then consider
  the US separately. Engage a real IP solicitor at that point.
- **Keep this file updated.** Re-run searches periodically (annually, or
  on a specific trigger) and add a new dated section above.
